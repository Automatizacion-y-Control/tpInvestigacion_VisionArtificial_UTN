# Referencias Documentales y Bibliografía

A continuación, se listan de manera consolidada las referencias formales de los fundamentos teóricos, ontológicos y dogmáticos que sustentan la construcción integral de la infraestructura AIoT según el formato IEEE:

[1] C. E. Shannon, "A Mathematical Theory of Communication," *The Bell System Technical Journal*, vol. 27, no. 3, pp. 379-423, Jul. 1948.
[2] L. Floridi, "The Ontological Interpretation of Informational Privacy," *Ethics and Information Technology*, vol. 7, no. 4, pp. 185-200, 2005.
[3] D. Poole y A. Mackworth, *Artificial Intelligence: Foundations of Computational Agents*, 3rd ed. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2023.
[4] Cisco IBSG, "The Internet of Things: How the Next Evolution of the Internet Is Changing Everything," Cisco, White Paper, 2012.
[5] P. Mell y T. Grance, "The NIST Definition of Cloud Computing," National Institute of Standards and Technology, Special Publication 800-145, Sept. 2011.
[6] ITU-T, "Overview of the Internet of things," Recommendation ITU-T Y.2060, Jun. 2012.
[7] OpenFog Consortium, "OpenFog Reference Architecture for Fog Computing," *IEEE*, 2017.
[8] ETSI, "Multi-access Edge Computing (MEC); Framework and Reference Architecture," ETSI GS MEC 003, V3.1.1, 2022.
[9] M. Weiser, "The Computer for the 21st Century," *Scientific American*, vol. 265, no. 3, pp. 94-104, Sept. 1991.
